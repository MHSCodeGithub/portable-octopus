Introduction

Welcome to GAME NAME! You have found yourself the king of a small kingdom. As a king you should do everything in your power to grow the kingdom and keep your citizens safe. Good luck!


Houses

Houses provide a place for citizens to sleep. Building houses or upgrading them will increase your citizen count. In order to have citizens you need to provide them with food, you can obtain food by using producers.


Producers

Producers produce commodities. There are several types of commodities, which includes food as referenced above. Some producers have an intake (a resource which they convert to another resource). For example, the mill converts wheat into flour. Producers require citizens in order to function. Producers also have tiers, which determine how many citizens are needed in order to operate the producer. The formula is as follows: workers = tier.

Functions

Producers can be upgraded or sold. In the case of houses, upgrading will increase the potential citizen count. The formula is as follows: citizens = house level * 5. In the case of producers, upgrading will increase the produce yield. If the producer has intake, it will also increase at the same ratio as produce.


Taxes

Both houses and producers provide taxes. The formula is as follows:
income per producer = (producer tier * 6) * producer level.


Starting Off

Though it all may be a bit confusing at first, we recommend a simple starting setup of a level 2 wheat farm and a level 1 house. This will provide you with 5 citizens, all fed, and a small income of wheat. Rember as you grow, to always ensure you have enough food for your citizens and enough housing to provide citizens for producers.
